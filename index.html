Crownsville Hospital

The Last Segregated Asylum

First Patients

The earliest patients were transferred from the Maryland Hospital for the insane and housed in a farm building. The pateints were tasked with the early construction of the new hospital.

Above image taken from the Twenty-Fifth Report of the Lunacy Commission

Name Change

In April 1910, the Hospital for the Negro Insane was created by an act of Maryland's General Assembly. However, in 1912 the hospital renamed itself from the Maryland Hospital for the Negro Insane to Crownsville State Hospital

Purpose of the Hospital

At the turn of the century in Maryland and elsewhere, many of these attitudes and worries about "insane" Black people were still lingering in public discourse. When Maryland's state assembly decided something must be done, they made clear that it needed to be done far away from Baltimore City.
